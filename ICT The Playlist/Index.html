THE PLAYLIST

The first episode of “The Playlist” is about the perspective of the founder of Spotify Daniel Ek.
It goes through the hardships he went through his life in order to make Spotify possible. Going
through negotiations with different record companies and failing to secure a deal repeatedly.

I like the style of storytelling they went with in which every episode is based on a
different point of view of one of the individuals that played a role in making Spotify.

Daniel Ek is portrayed as a young aspiring software engineer eager to make his
company Spotify work in the modern age with the changes of technology and culture seen in the
episode. Really shows how smart and innovative he is and how he does not give up easily when
faced with hardships.

It is very inspiring seeing someone go from having no job to being a multi-millionaire, we
could even say that he is a multi-billionaire. Thanks to his skills and smart thinking, he was able
to see the trends and problems happening in his time and how to also take advantage of them.

From what I can see now, Spotify really did its purpose of replacing and taking Piratebay
out of the picture. And to become the website a person would use when listening to music.
Because Piratebay currently is getting a fraction of the amount of traffic they used to get and
Spotify is now one of the largest music streaming services in the world.

Spotify dominates the music industry in the current years, a far cry from having no music
label wanting to sign with them in order to stream their music to the wider public. Spotify is so
commonly used nowadays that even I use Spotify to listen to not only music but also podcasts,
I am using Spotify as I write this paper.

In conclusion, this show really does teach us a lot of things. Like how the music industry
used to work and how it innovated from old times into the modern age, the characteristics of
